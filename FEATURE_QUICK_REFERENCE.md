## Common Patterns

### Pattern 1: Three-syllable words often show
- Stress (on one syllable)

- Rhythm (compressed syllables)

**Example**: photograph (F OW**1** T AH**0** G R AE**2** F)

## Tips for Learning

1. **Start with one feature**: Master stress before moving to t-flapping
2. **Use your ears**: Listen to native speakers on YouGlish
3. **Record yourself**: Compare to native pronunciation
4. **Practice daily**: 5-10 minutes consistently beats 1-hour cramming
5. **Focus on common words**: Master "water" before "photographic"
6. **Shadow native speakers**: Repeat immediately after hearing
7. **Exaggerate at first**: Overdo the feature, then tone it down
